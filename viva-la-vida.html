Viva la Vida

Frida Kahlo’s Life in Places, Pain and Painting

After first discovering Frida Kahlo through a film at age fifteen, her work has stayed with me. Earlier this year, “Frida Kahlo’s Month in Paris: A Friendship with Mary Reynolds” at the Art Institute of Chicago renewed that inspiration and sparked the idea for this map. This StoryMaps project follows her movements across cities, hospitals and homes, tracing the environments that shaped her art. I drew from museum archives, biographies and painting catalogues, then used the Tour Map feature in ArcGIS StoryMaps to guide viewers through her accidents, recoveries and creative milestones. The story concludes with a static map linking pain, painting and place, forming a geographic portrait of her resilience.
